Diversify Your Understanding

Triple Threat aims to educate its viewers about the intersectionality of both minorities and marginalized communities. There is a significant number of people who identify with one minority and also identify with another. However, for too long, these people have been shunned for doing so. Triple Threat is committed to breaking down societal norms surrounding intersectionality to create a safe space for those who identify within multiple aspects of the diversity spectrum. We hope our audience finds this website to be both engaging and informative, and that they take advantage of the multitude of resources available.

“The Intersectionality Wars” - Jane Coaston

The article explores how the concept of intersectionality, which acknowledges the ways in which different forms of oppression intersect, is often misunderstood and misused in conservative circles. Ultimately, the article argues that intersectionality, properly understood, can help to expose and address the ways in which multiple forms of oppression operate within society.

“COVID-19: Intersectionality Matters” - Lara Maestripierin

The article examines how political polarization erodes trust in government, media, and each other, with a focus on how it intersects with various social identities. The authors demonstrates the decline of trust among political individuals who belong to marginalized groups. They call for political and social interventions to rebuild trust, considering its intersectional nature.

“Teaching At The Intersections” - Monita K. Bell

The article advocates for the importance of intersectionality in education and provides examples of how teachers can incorporate it into their practice. The authors argue that an intersectional lens is essential to promote social justice and create a more inclusive and equitable learning environment.
